Pump Predictor — Volume Engine

🧬 Pattern Match Active
🧬 What drives a breakout — and what we scan for Updated daily
📈 Multi-day price climb
Steady grind higher, not a 1-day spike
The strongest setups don't gap once and reverse — price grinds steadily higher across several sessions, sometimes multiplying several times over in the process. A multi-day climb is far stickier than a single jump. This is the #1 signal we track (28%).
🔊 Volume vs. normal average
Multiple x a stock's usual daily volume
We compare today's volume to what's actually normal for that stock — a real climb shows volume running far above its usual average, which is what backs up the price move instead of leaving it unconfirmed. Weighted 18% as the supporting signal.
🌀 Float rotation
1x+ of the float traded
A "7x average volume" day means very different things on a 1M-share float vs. a 20M-share one. We convert volume into "how many times the entire float traded today" — that's the actual fuel behind a gap. Weighted 12%.
🏷️ Low float
Under 5M shares
Fewer shares outstanding means less supply has to be absorbed before price gaps. Weighted 9% on its own.
⚡ Short squeeze setup
Short% > 20%
High short interest plus a real price-and-volume move forces shorts to cover, creating a feedback loop that amplifies price moves violently. Weighted 6%.
🌙 After-hours move
+3% to +15% AH
A real climb often shows AH/pre-market follow-through the session before the bigger move — early positioning ahead of the open. Weighted 12%.
📋 Catalyst
News / SEC filing
A filing or news item often lights the fuse, but on its own a catalyst rarely moves a stock this much — it needs the price climb and volume behind it. Weighted 6%.
🆕 Freshness
Early in the move > already extended
A first big breakout off a long, quiet base tends to have more room to run than a name already several legs into the climb. Weighted 5%.
📞 Options flow
Unusual call-buying intensity
A spike in call activity can mean positioned money is leaning in ahead of a move, not just retail chasing the tape. Weighted 4%.
⚠️ Dilution risk (penalty)
Thin balance sheet → surprise offering risk
Names with weak fundamentals are more likely to get diluted right when they're hot, cutting a real move short. This subtracts up to 15 points rather than adding to the score.

How this works

Every stock below is scored daily by our Volume Engine. A score of 80+ means all major conditions align. The dominant input is a multi-day price climb — a steady grind higher across several sessions rather than a single spike (28%). That climb is then backed up by volume: how far today's volume runs above the stock's normal average (18%), and how much of the entire float traded today, since the same multiple-of-average means more on a tinier float (12%). Float size, short interest, after-hours follow-through, and catalyst strength add further supporting weight. Freshness and options flow add a smaller incremental lift, while dilution risk is subtracted as a straight penalty — a great setup on a balance sheet likely to get diluted is a real risk, not a footnote.
