Pump Predictor — Volume Engine

🧬 Pattern Match Active
🧬 What drives a breakout — and what we scan for Updated daily
📈 Multi-day price climb
Steady grind higher, not a 1-day spike
The strongest setups don't gap once and reverse — price grinds steadily higher across several sessions, sometimes multiplying several times over in the process. A multi-day climb is far stickier than a single jump. This is the #1 signal we track (30%).
🔊 Volume vs. normal average
Multiple x a stock's usual daily volume
We compare today's volume to what's actually normal for that stock — a real climb shows volume running far above its usual average, which is what backs up the price move instead of leaving it unconfirmed. Weighted 20% as the supporting signal.
🌀 Float rotation
1x+ of the float traded
A "7x average volume" day means very different things on a 1M-share float vs. a 20M-share one. We convert volume into "how many times the entire float traded today" — that's the actual fuel behind a gap. Weighted 13%.
🏷️ Low float
Under 5M shares
Fewer shares outstanding means less supply has to be absorbed before price gaps. Weighted 10% on its own.
⚡ Short squeeze setup
Short% > 20%
High short interest plus a real price-and-volume move forces shorts to cover, creating a feedback loop that amplifies price moves violently. Weighted 7%.
🌙 After-hours move
+3% to +15% AH
A real climb often shows AH/pre-market follow-through the session before the bigger move — early positioning ahead of the open. Weighted 13%.
📋 Catalyst
News / SEC filing
A filing or news item often lights the fuse, but on its own a catalyst rarely moves a stock this much — it needs the price climb and volume behind it. Weighted 7%.
🔬 Case study: AIIO (Robo.ai Inc.)
A real, recent example of the three signals above lining up at once — included so the pattern can be studied directly, not just described in the abstract. This is history, not a current recommendation.
🏷️ The base: AIIO spent late April into early May parked in a tight $0.60–$0.70 range for weeks — small candles, low attention, the quiet setup that often precedes an expansion move.
📈 The breakout & climb: Once it cleared $0.80 and then $1.00, the range opened up fast — closing near $1.13 on May 11, then $2.61 (intraday high $2.79) just two sessions later. That's a multi-day climb of several hundred percent, not a single one-off spike.
🚀 The peak: The climb kept extending over the following weeks, eventually printing highs above $9 — roughly 15x off the sub-$1 base — before sliding back hard into the $2–$3 range.
🔊 Volume confirmation: Each leg up came on a sharp jump in volume well above what was normal for the name — exactly the volume-vs-normal backup signal the engine looks for, rather than a quiet drift higher on thin trading.
🌙 After-hours/premarket: Several of the biggest days were preceded by premarket action — one session saw premarket trade push toward $3.60 and into the mid-$4s before the open, another saw a premarket spike near $5.87 ahead of the bell. That early positioning often foreshadowed the size of the day's move.
📉 The reversal: The move wasn't one-directional — by mid-June AIIO had given back most of the run, settling back into the $2–$3 area, and remains down sharply over the trailing year despite the spike.
⚠️ This is exactly the trade-off the Volume Engine is built to flag, not to dismiss: the multi-day price climb, volume confirmation, and after-hours follow-through are real, recurring signals worth studying — but they describe a setup that can unwind just as violently as it built. A high score is a reason to watch closely with a plan, never a reason to chase blindly.

How this works

Every stock below is scored daily by our Volume Engine. A score of 80+ means all major conditions align. The dominant input is a multi-day price climb — a steady grind higher across several sessions rather than a single spike (30%). That climb is then backed up by volume: how far today's volume runs above the stock's normal average (20%), and how much of the entire float traded today, since the same multiple-of-average means more on a tinier float (13%). Float size, short interest, after-hours follow-through, and catalyst strength make up the rest as supporting structural conditions, not standalone triggers.
